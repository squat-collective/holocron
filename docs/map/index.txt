14:T2020,<h1 id="3d-galaxy-map">3D galaxy map<a class="anchor" aria-label="Link to section" href="#3d-galaxy-map">#</a></h1>
<blockquote>
<p>"Google Maps for your data." Every entity is a star, every relationship is an edge, and the camera does the rest.</p>
</blockquote>
<p>The map lives at <a href="http://localhost:3333/?mode=map">http://localhost:3333/?mode=map</a> (or click the <strong>mode toggle</strong> on the home shell). The legacy <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>/map</span></span></code></span> route 301s into the home shell.</p>
<h2 id="what-it-shows">What it shows<a class="anchor" aria-label="Link to section" href="#what-it-shows">#</a></h2>
<ul>
<li><strong>Nodes</strong> — every asset, actor, and rule, coloured by entity type (datasets, reports, processes, systems, people, groups, rules).</li>
<li><strong>Edges</strong> — every relationship, coloured by type (<span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>owns</span></span></code></span>, <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>uses</span></span></code></span>, <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>feeds</span></span></code></span>, <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>contains</span></span></code></span>, <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>member_of</span></span></code></span>, <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>applies_to</span></span></code></span>).</li>
<li><strong>Animated particles</strong> flow along edges in the direction of the relationship — you can read lineage at a glance.</li>
<li><strong>Size</strong> scales with degree (popular nodes look bigger).</li>
</ul>
<p>The graph is laid out <strong>once at API startup</strong> with NetworkX <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>spring_layout</span></span></code></span> and cached in memory. Coordinates are stable across reads; the UI gets pre-computed <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>(x, y, z)</span></span></code></span> per node.</p>
<h2 id="levels-of-detail">Levels of detail<a class="anchor" aria-label="Link to section" href="#levels-of-detail">#</a></h2>
<p><span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>GET /api/v1/graph/map?lod={0|1}</span></span></code></span>:</p>
<ul>
<li><strong><span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>lod=0</span></span></code></span></strong> — overview: only systems and teams. The "tectonic" view.</li>
<li><strong><span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>lod=1</span></span></code></span></strong> — full graph: + datasets, reports, processes, people, rules.</li>
</ul>
<p>The UI defaults to <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>lod=1</span></span></code></span> and lets you toggle.</p>

<h2 id="search--map">Search ↔ map<a class="anchor" aria-label="Link to section" href="#search--map">#</a></h2>
<ul>
<li>The home shell has both modes; switching to map keeps the current search query.</li>
<li>Filtering search down to a kind (<span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>ds:</span></span></code></span>, <span data-rehype-pretty-code-figure=""><code data-language="plaintext" data-theme="github-dark-dimmed github-light"><span data-line=""><span>r:</span></span></code></span>) also filters what's visible in the map.</li>
<li>Selecting a search hit centres the camera on that node.</li>
</ul>

<h2 id="limitations">Limitations<a class="anchor" aria-label="Link to section" href="#limitations">#</a></h2>
<ul>
<li>Layout is computed once at startup. Adding many entities later doesn't re-layout — they get positioned but the global topology drifts. Restart the API to re-layout.</li>
<li>At very large scale (>50k nodes) the 3D renderer slows. The product target is "tens of thousands of nodes"; beyond that, plan to filter or sample before rendering.</li>
<li>The map is a single-tenant view. There's no per-user filter or workspace concept.</li>
